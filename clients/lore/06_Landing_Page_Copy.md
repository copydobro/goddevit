## [PROBLEM SECTION]

### Section label
```
THE COST OF LOSING IT
```

### Body
```
You're deep in flow. A thought fires. You switch to your notes app,
wait for it to load, decide where to put the idea, lose the thread
you were following.

Multiply that by 20 times a day. That's the friction tax you pay
to every tool that makes you organize before you can think.

The thought is gone before the folder is chosen.
```

## [HOW IT WORKS]

### Section label
```
THREE SECONDS
```

### Section headline
```
Capture. Store. Recall.
```

### Step 1 — Capture
**Title:** Capture

**Body:**
```
Press ⌘⇧Space anywhere on your Mac.
A panel appears. Type anything.
Press Enter. Close. Back to work.

You never left your app.
```

### Step 2 — Store
**Title:** Store locally

**Body:**
```
Lore vectorizes your thought using Ollama,
running entirely on your machine.
LanceDB stores it locally.

No file. No folder. No decision required.
No internet connection needed.
```

### Step 3 — Recall
**Title:** Recall

**Body:**
```
Ask in plain language.
"What was that thing about the API rate limits?"

Lore surfaces it. Semantically, not by keyword.
Your own memory, returned to you.
```
